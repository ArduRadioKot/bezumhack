About the Company

LLC "Tmyv deneg" – a company founded by a legendary (in narrow circles) personality named Vitalik "Lux" Karlikov. Vitalik is a former circus performer (height 127 cm) who became famous in the 2010s as the shortest illusionist in the post-Soviet space. After the circus closed down, he retrained as an info-guru: selling courses like "How to Build Passive Income in 3 Days" and "Secrets of Manipulating Wealthy People." The scheme brought him a fortune but left him with a reputation as a fraudster. In 2023, Vitalik decided it was time to "legitimize" and create something truly luxurious. Thus was born the idea of LUXARY – an elite marketplace where you can buy yachts, planes, islands, and even space tours. "I can't reach the steering wheel myself, but I can sell a yacht to someone who can," he likes to say. Vitalik entrusted the platform development to his nephew Grisha – a student programmer who learned from YouTube videos and devoutly believed that any database query could be assembled through string concatenation. Grisha also insisted that all passwords be stored in plain text "for support convenience." Vitalik, not understanding IT, signed off on everything Grisha proposed. The company's main feature is an unusual motivation system: the more bugs the testing team finds, the higher the bonus. But after the testers quit due to delayed wages, Vitalik announced an "open bounty" for everyone on the internet. Now anyone can try to hack the platform and receive not only recognition but also (according to Vitalik) "the opportunity to become his personal assistant for elite relations." In reality, after discovering vulnerabilities, he plans to simply incorporate them into his new courses "Hacking for Millionaires." Vitalik himself prefers to manage the business from his apartment, decorated with golden toilets and posters of his own face. Rumor has it his admin password is qwerty123 because "wealthy people don't use complex passwords." During the hackathon, participants will have to prove that even the most ridiculous business can become a victim of serious cyber threats.
